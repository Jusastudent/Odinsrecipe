Red Braised Tiger Skin Eggs

Boiled Eggs Evil Twin

These tiger skin eggs are more known in asian cultures. They may not be the ooey gooey sunny side up eggs that many have become accustom too. These have their own use as a sponge absorbing all the flavors around it. This version was created by Chinese Cooking Demystified.

    Ingredients

  • 6 Eggs
  • 1 Garlic cloves
  • 1/2inch piece of Ginger
  • 1 Whole Clove
  • 1/8 Cinnamon Stick/Cassia stick
  • 1 Dried Bay Leaf
  • 1/4 teaspoon Sichuah peppercorns
  • Shaoxing Wine or Wine of your choice
  • 1 cup Water (H2O)
  • 2 Tbsp Light Soy Sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon Dark Soy Sauce
  • Slurry 2 teaspoons of each Cornstach and Water (H2O)
  • 2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon Salt
  • 1/8 tsp MSG
  • 1 teaspoon Sesame Oil
  • a Handfull of Chopped Scallions

    Cooking

  1. Small pot, medium flame, swirl in ~1tbsp of oil and fry the ginger & garlic til fragrant, ~30 seconds.
  2. Add the spices, fry ~15 seconds. Add in the eggs, then swirl in the wine. Quick mix.
  3. Add in the water, soy sauces, and seasoning. Bring to a boil, swap the flame down to medium. Keep at a light boil, uncovered.
  4. Flip the eggs a few times through the cooking process. Let it cook for ~12 minutes, or til the liquid's reduced by about one third.
  5. Add the MSG and the slurry. Let it thicken, ~30 seconds.
  6. eat off, drizzle in the sesame oil & add the scallions.
